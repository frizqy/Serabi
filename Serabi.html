Serabi

Serabi is a traditional snack originating from Indnesian. Serabi is often eaten for breakfast because many food stalls selling Serabi as breakfast Sometimes Serabi is also sold at night.

What Is Serabi?

Usually serabi is classified as a sweet food with a sweet taste, therefore serabi has many sweet toppings such as sugar, coconut, peanuts, bananas, and chocolate. Serabi is similar to pancakes but what distinguishes pancakes from pancakes is the difference, pancake batter uses rice flour while pancakes use wheat flour. Serabi requires coconut milk in the batter, while pancakes don't use coconut milk.

Recipe Serabi

First Mix the rice flour, starch and wheat flour in a bowl. Then add sugar and salt after that mix well. Enter the liquid coconut milk little by little while continuing to stir until it forms a dough. Then, add the thick coconut milk, stir until smooth. Heat a pancake or Teflon mold, grease it with coconut pulp so it doesn't stick. Pour a spoonful of serabi, then cover. Cook until done.

Kinca Sause

Kinca is used to add sweetness to have a liquid texture. Kinca is widely used in various foods, especially for Serabi. Kinca also has another flavor variant, namely Kinca Durian.

Recipes Kinca

To make kinca sauce, you only need palm sugar, coconut milk and pandan leaves (as food fragrance). The first thing to do is to melt the palm sugar with a little water and add the pandan leaves, after the palm sugar melts add the coconut milk and stir until it boils. when done, Kinca sauce is ready and can be eaten or used for pancakes.
